Frequently Asked Questions

1. What size dumpster do I need for my project?

The right size depends on your specific project type and volume of waste. A 10-yard dumpster typically handles small cleanouts and minor renovations. 20-yard containers work well for medium bathroom or kitchen remodels and garage cleanouts. 30-yard dumpsters accommodate larger renovation projects and estate cleanouts. 40-yard containers are designed for major construction, demolition, or commercial projects. Our team can assess your needs over the phone and recommend the most cost-effective size based on your description.

2. How quickly can you deliver a dumpster?

In most areas across the United States, we offer same-day or next-day delivery when you call before noon. Delivery times vary by location and seasonal demand, but we prioritize urgent requests whenever possible. For scheduled projects, booking 2-3 days in advance ensures your preferred delivery window.

3. What items cannot go in your dumpsters?

We cannot accept hazardous materials including paint, chemicals, asbestos, batteries, tires, electronics, refrigerants, or medical waste. These items require specialized disposal. We can, however, handle most construction debris, household junk, yard waste, furniture, appliances (with refrigerants removed), and general trash. When in doubt, call us with a specific list and we'll clarify what's acceptable.

4. How long can I keep the dumpster?

Our standard rental period is 7-10 days, which accommodates most residential and commercial projects. If you need the container longer, we offer flexible extensions at reasonable daily rates. For estate cleanouts or complex renovations, we can arrange multi-week rentals upfront. Just let us know your timeline when booking.

5. Do I need a permit to place a dumpster on my property?

Permits are typically not required when placing a dumpster on private property like your driveway or construction site. However, if you need to place the container on a public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way, most municipalities require a permit. Requirements vary significantly by city and county. We can provide guidance for your specific location and in many cases help facilitate the permit process.
